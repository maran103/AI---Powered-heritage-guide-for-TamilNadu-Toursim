Brihadeeswarar Temple:
The Brihadeeswarar Temple in Thanjavur was built by Raja Raja Chola I during the 11th century. It is a UNESCO World Heritage Site famous for its massive vimana tower, granite construction, and Dravidian architecture. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and represents the architectural brilliance of the Chola dynasty.

Meenakshi Amman Temple:
Meenakshi Amman Temple in Madurai is dedicated to Goddess Meenakshi and Lord Sundareswarar. The temple is known for its colorful gopurams, intricate sculptures, and large temple complex. It is one of the most visited pilgrimage sites in South India.

Shore Temple:
The Shore Temple in Mahabalipuram was built during the Pallava dynasty in the 8th century. It overlooks the Bay of Bengal and is one of the oldest structural stone temples in South India. The temple is recognized as part of the UNESCO World Heritage Site at Mahabalipuram.

Ramanathaswamy Temple:
Ramanathaswamy Temple in Rameswaram is one of the twelve Jyotirlinga temples dedicated to Lord Shiva. It is famous for its long corridors, sacred water tanks, and religious significance in Hindu mythology related to Lord Rama.

Airavatesvara Temple:
The Airavatesvara Temple in Darasuram was built by Rajaraja Chola II. It is known for its stone carvings, chariot-shaped architecture, and detailed sculptures. The temple is part of the UNESCO Great Living Chola Temples.

Nataraja Temple:
The Nataraja Temple in Chidambaram is dedicated to Lord Shiva as the cosmic dancer Nataraja. The temple is an important center of Shaivism and is famous for its golden roof and spiritual significance related to dance and cosmic energy.

Ekambareswarar Temple:
Ekambareswarar Temple in Kanchipuram is one of the Pancha Bhoota temples representing the element Earth. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is famous for its massive gopuram and ancient mango tree believed to be thousands of years old.

Kamakshi Amman Temple:
Kamakshi Amman Temple in Kanchipuram is dedicated to Goddess Kamakshi, a form of Parvati. It is one of the most important Shakti temples in India and attracts thousands of devotees every year.

Varadaraja Perumal Temple:
Varadaraja Perumal Temple in Kanchipuram is dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Built during the Chola and Vijayanagara periods, the temple is known for its detailed carvings, stone pillars, and sacred lizard sculptures.

Srivilliputhur Andal Temple:
The Srivilliputhur Andal Temple is dedicated to Goddess Andal and Lord Vishnu. The temple tower is featured in the official emblem of the Tamil Nadu government and is an important Vaishnavite pilgrimage center.

Jambukeswarar Temple:
Jambukeswarar Temple in Tiruchirappalli is one of the Pancha Bhoota temples representing the element Water. The temple is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is famous for its underground water stream beneath the sanctum.

Annamalaiyar Temple:
Annamalaiyar Temple in Tiruvannamalai is dedicated to Lord Shiva and represents the fire element among the Pancha Bhoota temples. The temple is associated with the Karthigai Deepam festival celebrated with a giant lamp on Arunachala hill.

Murugan Temple Palani:
The Palani Murugan Temple is one of the six abodes of Lord Murugan. Located on a hill in Palani, the temple attracts millions of pilgrims and is famous for its Panchamirtham prasadam.

Kapaleeshwarar Temple:
Kapaleeshwarar Temple in Chennai is dedicated to Lord Shiva and reflects classic Dravidian architecture. The temple is known for its colorful gopuram and the annual Arubathimoovar festival.

Parthasarathy Temple:
Parthasarathy Temple in Chennai is dedicated to Lord Krishna as Parthasarathy, the charioteer of Arjuna. It is one of the oldest temples in Chennai and belongs to the Vaishnavite tradition.

Ranganathaswamy Temple:
The Sri Ranganathaswamy Temple in Srirangam is dedicated to Lord Vishnu and is one of the largest functioning temple complexes in the world. The temple has massive gopurams and significant importance in Vaishnavism.

Kumbeswarar Temple:
Kumbeswarar Temple in Kumbakonam is dedicated to Lord Shiva and is associated with the legend of the cosmic pot during creation. The temple is an important pilgrimage site during the Mahamaham festival.

Suchindram Thanumalayan Temple:
The Thanumalayan Temple in Suchindram is dedicated to the trinity of Shiva, Vishnu, and Brahma. The temple is known for its musical pillars and detailed sculptures.

Nellaiappar Temple:
Nellaiappar Temple in Tirunelveli is dedicated to Lord Shiva and Goddess Kanthimathi. The temple complex is famous for its musical pillars, large mandapams, and traditional architecture.

Thillai Kali Temple:
The Thillai Kali Temple in Chidambaram is dedicated to Goddess Kali. According to legend, the temple is associated with the dance competition between Lord Shiva and Goddess Kali.
